What Paul and Peter Saw in the Future

A plainspoken reflection for the weary, the teachable, and the cast-out.

Using Paul's letters to the churches, speculate on what he saw in the future

Paul’s Vision (For the Weary Soul)

1. People Would Twist the Gospel: Paul knew some would turn the good news into something heavier—rules, pressure, confusion. But grace never changes. If you’re holding on to Jesus, that’s enough.

2. A Remnant Would Remain: Even if churches drift, Paul believed some folks would keep clinging to Christ—quiet, steady, unseen by the world but known by God.

3. The Church Would Be Bigger Than Expected: Not just one group or one place. Paul saw Jews and Gentiles together, rich and poor, all adopted into the same family.

4. There Would Be Spiritual Battle: He didn’t hide it. Walking with Jesus invites resistance. But Paul gave us armor: truth, faith, salvation. Not to impress, but to endure.

5. Jesus Is Coming Back: Paul’s greatest hope? That Jesus would return and set things right. The weary would rise. The faithful would be gathered. The waiting would end.

Did the Apostle Peter have views on the same topic?

Peter’s Vision (For the One Who Feels Left Out)

1. Outsiders Would Be Welcomed In: Peter saw God’s heart through a vision — He was opening the door wide. The gospel was for anyone with faith.

2. The Spirit Moves Freely: When Gentiles received the Spirit, Peter knew God didn’t wait for “clean” credentials. Just open hearts.

3. Suffering Would Be Part of the Story: Peter said don’t be surprised by trials — they refine you, not reject you.

4. Believers Are Living Stones: Not a temple with walls — but a spiritual house built with lives, even the cracked ones.

5. The Return of Jesus Means Justice: Peter’s hope was the same: a new heaven, a new earth, a place where righteousness finally feels like home.
