What Paul and Peter Saw in the Future

Using Paul's letters to the churches, speculate on what he saw in the future

Paul’s Vision (For the Weary and Teachable)

1. People Would Twist the Gospel: Paul warned not everyone would stick to the truth. Some would burden others with rules or confusion. He urged believers to hold tight to grace alone.

2. A Remnant Would Remain: Even if things got messy, some would still love Jesus deeply—quietly walking in faith while others drifted.

3. The Church Would Cross Borders: He saw a global body—different races, classes, and cultures knit together as one family in Christ.

4. A Spiritual Struggle Would Unfold: There’d be opposition—not just social, but spiritual. He urged believers to stand firm, armored not in pride but in truth, faith, and grace.

5. Jesus Will Return: Paul kept his eyes on the horizon. Christ's return wasn’t a fear tactic—it was a promise of restoration and reunion.

Did the Apostle Peter have views on the same topic?

Peter’s Vision (For the One Who Feels Left Out)

1. The Family Would Get Bigger: Peter saw walls come down. His vision at Joppa revealed that God was welcoming people far outside the expected religious mold.

2. The Spirit Moves Freely: When Gentiles received the Spirit, Peter knew God didn’t wait for “clean” credentials. He responds to hearts open in faith.

3. Suffering Would Come: Peter didn’t paint it rosy. He knew fire would come, but he called it refining—not rejection.

4. Every Believer Was a Living Stone: Whether cracked or polished, each believer had a place in God's spiritual house.

5. Jesus Is Coming to Make Things Right: Peter joined Paul in looking forward. Justice, righteousness, and hope are coming—and we’re invited in.

“You’re not disqualified. You’re exactly the kind of person God wants to use.”
